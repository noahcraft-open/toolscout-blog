## What is Keigo and Why Does It Matter?

Keigo (敬語) is the formal, respectful language system used in Japanese business communication. It is not simply "polite Japanese" — it is an entirely different register with its own vocabulary, verb forms, and grammar structures. Using incorrect keigo in a business email can seriously damage professional relationships.

There are three main levels:

- **Sonkeigo (尊敬語)**: Respectful language used to elevate the listener's actions
- **Kenjōgo (謙譲語)**: Humble language used to lower your own actions
- **Teineigo (丁寧語)**: Polite language — the most commonly used in everyday formal writing

Getting the balance right requires deep cultural and linguistic knowledge. That is where AI tools come in.

## Tips for Better Keigo Emails

Even with AI assistance, these tips will improve your results:

### 1. Be Specific With Context
The more specific information you provide (company name, your relationship with the recipient, urgency level), the more appropriate the AI-generated keigo will be.

### 2. Verify Recipient Names
Japanese business emails require correct honorifics (様, 御中, etc.). Make sure you use 様 for individuals and 御中 for organizations.

### 3. Understand the Structure
A standard Japanese business email follows this structure:
1. **Recipient's name** with 様 or 御中
2. **Company name and your name** (自己紹介)
3. **Opening pleasantry** (お世話になっております etc.)
4. **Main body** (本文)
5. **Closing** (よろしくお願いいたします etc.)
6. **Signature block** (署名)

### 4. Adapt for Your Industry
Some industries (finance, law, government) require more formal keigo than others (tech startups, creative agencies). Provide this context in the tool.
