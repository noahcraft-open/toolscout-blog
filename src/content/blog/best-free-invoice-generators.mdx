## What Information Should Be on Every Invoice?

A professional invoice should always include these essential elements:

### Your Business Information

Include your full business name, address, phone number, and email address. If you have a business registration number or tax ID, include that as well.

### Client Information

Always include your client's name, company name (if applicable), and billing address. This ensures the invoice reaches the right person and department.

### Invoice Details

Every invoice needs a unique invoice number for tracking purposes. Include the invoice date and the due date for payment. Be specific about your payment terms (Net 15, Net 30, etc.).

### Line Items

Break down your charges into individual line items with clear descriptions. Include quantities, rates, and subtotals for each item. This transparency builds trust with clients.

### Payment Information

Specify how you want to be paid (bank transfer, PayPal, credit card, etc.) and include any necessary payment details like bank account information or payment links.

## Common Invoicing Mistakes to Avoid

### Not Following Up on Late Payments

Many freelancers feel awkward about following up on unpaid invoices. Set up a system: send a reminder at 7 days overdue, 14 days, and 30 days. Most invoice generators can automate this process.

### Inconsistent Invoice Formatting

Using different formats for different clients looks unprofessional. Choose one tool and stick with it. Your invoices should be instantly recognizable as coming from your business.

### Forgetting to Save Copies

Always save copies of your invoices for tax purposes and record-keeping. Most free invoice generators let you download PDFs—make sure you do so for every invoice.

### Not Including Payment Terms

Vague payment expectations lead to late payments. Be specific: "Payment due within 30 days of invoice date" is much better than "Payment due upon receipt."
